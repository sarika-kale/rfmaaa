    What is RFM analysis? Why RFM for Segmentation?
    Recency, Frequency, and Monetary (RFM) analysis is used to represent customer behavior characteristics. RFM models have been used in direct marketing for more than 30 years. Research strongly suggests that past purchases of consumers are better predictors of their future purchase behavior than demographics. RFM Analysis divides consumers into segments based on the value of their purchases (Monetary), the frequency of their purchases (Frequency), and the time since last purchase (Recency). Given these segments, businesses can identify which customers are more important to their business, and and efficiently target them based on their behaviour patterns. This is explained further under section Data Visualization.
